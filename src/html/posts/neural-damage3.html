Neural Damage - DeepCon Edition

01/09/2022

Digital Painting / Collage

Taking a different look on destruction as procees of art creation is what gave life to this series. Fascination by patterns we are unconsciously faced with on daily basis was key inspiriation - from layers of street tags to damaged roads and scraped buildings. If it's difficult to appreciate the esthetics of it, we can appreciate life as a expression at least. As each of the elements can be viewed also as a sign of living, because without expression and creation it is just flow of time what defines ours surrondings. Damage is term associated with destruction often, in the eyes of design however it can play major role when deciding what is pleasant to our taste. With upcoming decades we might see big growth of the movement as it prefers to re-create and use already existing elements. Also, methods used are often term of interested for random viewer which might find the process as interesting as the result itself. What we can witness as well nowadays is rather opposite journey of artwork in which is the creation itself is major term of interested with result often not so satisfying to experienced eyeballs.


Details





On the edge of two movements? Being the first one representation for artwork as point of interest and with second one celebrating the process of creation. What could be seen as fascintating is almost exact opposite distinction between two movements in physical world. Subject number one with ink marker, spray can within public space. Action from few seconds to hours is still very quick in terms of resulting longetivity. Second subject on the other side focused days, weeks, months in the process with result scrapped right after it's done or ending with no deeper value.














Variations

Darker perspective of this work allowed to emphatise background urban atmosphere and earthy color contrasts. This is only collage work from this series, as it's fed off paper elements. After deeper look you might also find a bit of bloody tones coming from behind.


Original work, it set the ground for other color variants with dynamic background strokes, contrasting to geometrical shapes in different layers and shades.
